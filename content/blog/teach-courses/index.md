This work was developed as part of a broader project focused on urban water management and climate resilience in the San Salvador Metropolitan Area (AMSS).

The project, implemented by OPAMSS within the EUROCLIMA+ program, aimed to strengthen the management of the urban water cycle through hydrogeological analysis, capacity building, and the development of strategies for sustainable groundwater use and climate change adaptation.

It contributes to national and metropolitan efforts to reduce vulnerability, improve water availability, and promote integrated water resource management.


---

## 🌊 Project Overview

The initiative focused on improving the understanding and management of the hydrological cycle in urban environments, particularly groundwater systems.

Key components included:

- Development of hydrogeological, recharge, vulnerability, and contamination risk maps  
- Strengthening technical and institutional capacities  
- Implementation of pilot projects for groundwater protection  
- Promotion of sustainable urban drainage systems  

The project supports climate adaptation strategies by improving planning tools and encouraging more sustainable use of water resources.

## 🎧 Podcast Episode

As part of the project’s outreach and communication efforts, a podcast series (*Diálogos por el Agua*) was developed to create accessible spaces for discussing water-related challenges and solutions.

### Episode: *Ecosystems in the Water Cycle*

This third episode focuses on the influence of ecosystem conservation in ensuring water availability and quality, highlighting the connection between natural systems and sustainable water management.

The discussion features:

- **Jorge Castañeda**, President of the Environmental Fund of El Salvador (FONAES), Civil Engineer with a Master's degree in Hydraulic Resources (Hydrology)  
- **Jorge Oviedo**, Executive Director of the Environmental Investment Fund of El Salvador (FIAES), Agronomist with a Master's in Integrated Natural Resource Management  

**My role:** I served as the moderator of the session, facilitating the discussion and guiding the exchange between experts.

The episode explores how ecosystem conservation contributes to maintaining water quality and availability, emphasizing the need to integrate environmental protection into urban water management strategies, particularly under climate change pressures.
