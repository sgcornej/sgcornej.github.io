This project explores how surface flooding can impact sanitary sewer systems and contribute to inflow and infiltration (I&I). 
I developed a visualization combining a high-resolution DEM, sewer network data, and simulated water depth to identify areas
where flooding may compromise infrastructure.e.

The map integrates results from a hydrologic simulation (SIMWE in GRASS GIS) with elevation and sewer system data to 
highlight vulnerable zones. The goal is to improve understanding of how climate-driven precipitation and flooding can 
increase stress on wastewater systems and lead to potential environmental and public health hazards.

This work contributes to ongoing research on I&I by linking environmental processes with infrastructure performance through
geospatial visualization.
